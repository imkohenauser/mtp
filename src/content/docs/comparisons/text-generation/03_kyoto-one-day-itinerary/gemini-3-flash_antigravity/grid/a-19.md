Incidentally, walking from there down the Philosopher’s Path, you might find your thoughts wandering as much as the trail itself. It’s too hot to rush. In the summer, they set up *Kawadoko*—wooden platforms built directly over the rushing Kibune River. You eat your lunch just inches above the water, and the temperature drops five degrees instantly. It’s a literal escape. You absolutely must verify reservations for *Kawadoko* dining in advance, as the popular spots like Hirobun (famous for their *nagashi somen*—flowing noodles) fill up weeks ahead.

The water at Kibune-jinja is said to have its own spirit. You buy a *mizu-ura mikuji*, a paper fortune that is blank until you float it on the shrine's sacred spring. It makes me think of how much of Kyoto is hidden until you look at it through the right lens. 

As the sun begins to dip, head back towards Gion. The humidity makes the old wooden buildings of Hanami-koji smell of cedar and history.
