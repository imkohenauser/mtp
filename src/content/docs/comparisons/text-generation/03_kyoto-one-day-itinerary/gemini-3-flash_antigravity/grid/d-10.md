#### Thermal Management

### 3. Background: The Cultural Roots of Kyoto Summer

#### Kawadoko Culture
The tradition of dining over water dates back to the Edo period. In Kibune, it is a refined, quiet experience, whereas along the Kamo River, it is more social and vibrant. Both reflect the Japanese philosophy of *Yūgen* (subtle grace) found in nature's elements.

#### Gion Matsuri Context
If your visit falls in July, the entire city is dominated by the Gion Matsuri. Even on non-parade days, the *Yoiyama* (evening festivals) feature massive *Yamaboko* floats displayed on the streets, accompanied by traditional *Kon-chiki-chin* music.
