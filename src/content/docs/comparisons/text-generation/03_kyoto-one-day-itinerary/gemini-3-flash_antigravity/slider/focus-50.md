### Critical Definitions and Verification Requirements

**1. Kawadoko (川床) - Riverside Dining**
Kawadoko refers to the traditional Kyoto practice of dining on wooden platforms built over or beside water to utilize natural cooling. In the Kibune area, these platforms are positioned just inches above the river, providing a significant temperature drop compared to the city center.
*   **Advance Verification**: **Reservations are essential.** Popular venues like *Hirobun* or *Kibunesou* book up weeks in advance for lunch during July and August. 
*   **Note**: If you prefer "Nagashi Somen" (flowing noodles), these are often first-come, first-served and do not accept reservations, resulting in wait times of 1–2 hours.

**2. Mizuura-mikuji (水占いみくじ) - Water Fortune**
A unique ritual at Kifune Shrine where fortunes are printed on slips that appear blank until placed on the shrine's sacred spring water.
*   **Advance Verification**: While the shrine grounds are open from 06:00, the amulet office (where fortunes are sold) typically operates from **09:00 to 17:00**. 
*   **Tanabata Note**: Kifune often hosts "Tanabata" (Star Festival) illuminations until 20:00 during early August; check the 2026 schedule for extended evening access.

**3. Kodai-ji Summer Night Viewing (夏の夜間特別拝観)**
