### Early Morning: The Whispering Bamboo of Arashiyama
Start your day at **Arashiyama** as early as possible (around 7:30 AM). The **Sagano Bamboo Forest** is significantly cooler in the morning, and the shade of the towering stalks provides a natural reprieve. ### Late Morning: The Golden Reflection
Head to **Kinkaku-ji (The Golden Pavilion)**. While it can be crowded, the sight of the gold leaf reflecting off the Kyoko-chi pond is a summer staple. The walk through the grounds is relatively quick, allowing you to move onward before the midday sun reaches its peak.

### Midday & Afternoon: The Cool Escape of Kibune
This is the "special" heart of your itinerary. Take the Eizan Railway from Demachiyanagi Station to **Kibune**, a small village in the northern mountains. 
*   **Kawadoko Dining:** In summer, restaurants in Kibune build wooden platforms over the rushing Kibune River. You can enjoy a multi-course *kaiseki* lunch or *nagashi somen* (flowing noodles) just inches above the water. The temperature here is often 5–10°C lower than in central Kyoto.
*   **Kifune Shrine:** Visit the shrine known for its water deity. Try the *mizu-ura mikuji* (water-fortune), where the text appears only when the paper is dipped into the sacred spring.

### Evening: Lanterns and Evening Breezes
Return to the city as the sun begins to set and head to the **Gion District**. Walk through **Hanamikoji Street** toward **Yasaka Shrine**. In the summer, the shrine is beautifully illuminated with lanterns, and the "Gion Matsuri" atmosphere (especially in July) permeates the air. End your day with a stroll along the **Kamo River**, where you’ll see the *Yuka* (elevated decks) of Pontocho restaurants reflecting in the water, a final reminder of Kyoto's mastery over the summer heat.

*   **Temple Closures:** Most temples, including Kinkaku-ji and Tenryu-ji, close their gates between 4:30 PM and 5:00 PM.
*   **Summer Festivals:** If your visit coincides with July 17th or 24th (Gion Matsuri Yamaboko Junko) or August 16th (Daimonji Gozan no Okuribi), transport and crowds will be significantly different.
