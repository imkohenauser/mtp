## The Route

**Morning — Fushimi Inari → Nishiki Market**

Start at **Fushimi Inari Taisha** before 7 a.m. This is the move. The famous vermilion torii tunnels fill with tour groups by 9 a.m.; you'll have them nearly to yourself at dawn. Climb as far as the upper shrine for the view, then descend and take the train north to Shijo.

Walk **Nishiki Market** for breakfast — grilled skewers, tamagoyaki, fresh tofu. It opens around 9–10 a.m. depending on the stall.

**Midday — Gion → Kenninji**

Walk east into **Gion**. The machiya townhouses on Hanamikoji Street photograph best before noon when the light is flat and crowds are low. Duck into **Kenninji** (Kyoto's oldest Zen temple) — the twin dragon ceiling and dry gardens are exceptional and undervisited. ¥600 admission. Closes at 5 p.m.

Take cover during the midday heat (noon–2 p.m.) — this is your best window for lunch. **Gion Nanba** or any ramen shop nearby. In summer, the heat in Kyoto is serious; plan a cold meal and a rest.

**Afternoon — Philosopher's Path → Nanzenji**

Take a taxi or bus north to **Heian Shrine** for the gardens, then walk south along the **Philosopher's Path** to **Nanzenji**. The aqueduct at Nanzenji is one of the most distinctive structures in the city. From Nanzenji, continue to the sub-temple **Tenjuan** for its pond garden. Nanzenji grounds are free; Tenjuan is ¥500.

**Evening — Gion again, or Pontocho**

Return to **Pontocho Alley** at dusk. Reserve dinner in advance — this is non-negotiable in summer, as the best restaurants along the canal fill weeks ahead. If you want to see a **maiko or geiko**, linger near Gion's Hanamikoji from 6–8 p.m. They walk to engagements; you'll spot them.
