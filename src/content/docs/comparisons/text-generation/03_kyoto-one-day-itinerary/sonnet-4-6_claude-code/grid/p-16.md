## One-Day Special Itinerary: Kyoto in Summer

### Morning

**7:00 AM — Fushimi Inari Taisha**
Start before the crowds and heat. Walk the full tunnel of vermilion torii gates up the mountain — the upper trails are peaceful at dawn. The shrine is open 24/7, no reservation needed. *Summer note: bring water; it gets hot by 9 AM.*

**9:30 AM — Nishiki Market ("Kyoto's Kitchen")**
Stroll through and sample local street food — grilled skewers, pickled vegetables, fresh tofu. Shops typically open around 9–10 AM. **Verify hours in advance**, as some vendors close mid-week.

---

### Midday

**11:00 AM — Nijo Castle**
Gorgeous Edo-period architecture, famous "nightingale floors," and expansive gardens. Usually open 8:45 AM–5 PM; **closed on Tuesdays** (confirm for your specific dates). Tickets purchasable at the gate, but busy summer periods may warrant arriving early.

**1:00 PM — Lunch in Gion**
Head east to Gion, Kyoto's historic geisha district. Try kaiseki-style set lunch (far more affordable than dinner). **Reservation strongly recommended** for any sit-down restaurant; book 1–2 weeks ahead.

---

### Afternoon

**2:30 PM — Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)**
The iconic gold-leaf temple reflecting in its pond is unmissable. Open ~9 AM–5 PM; no advance reservation needed, but it gets crowded — aim to arrive mid-afternoon when tour buses thin out. *Be ready for heat with no shade near the main viewing area.*

**4:00 PM — Ryoan-ji (Rock Garden)**
A 10-minute walk or short bus ride from Kinkaku-ji. The Zen rock garden rewards slow, quiet contemplation — a sharp contrast to the bustle of the morning. Open ~8 AM–5 PM in summer. **Check for seasonal hour extensions.**

---

### Evening

**6:00 PM — Philosopher's Path & Nanzen-ji**
The canal-side stone path is beautiful in evening light. Nanzen-ji (including the aqueduct arch) is free to enter the grounds; the main hall has a small fee. **Check last-entry times** (~5–5:30 PM for paid areas).

**7:30 PM — Dinner in Pontocho**
This narrow lantern-lit alley along the Kamogawa River is one of Kyoto's great evening experiences. Many restaurants offer *kawadoko* (riverside terrace dining) in summer — a genuine seasonal specialty. **Reservation essential**, ideally 2–4 weeks in advance for a good riverside seat.

---
