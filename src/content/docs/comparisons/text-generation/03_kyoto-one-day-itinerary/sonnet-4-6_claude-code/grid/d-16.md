## One-Day Special Kyoto Itinerary (Summer)

**Assumptions:** You have a full day (roughly 8 AM–9 PM), are comfortable walking, and want a mix of iconic landmarks and atmosphere. Summer means heat/humidity, crowds, and some unique events (Gion Matsuri in July, Daimonji in August).

---

### Phase 1 — Morning (8:00–12:00): Eastern Higashiyama

Start early to beat the heat and crowds.

- **8:00** Fushimi Inari Taisha — walk the lower torii gates before tour buses arrive. The full summit takes ~2 hrs; going 30–45 min up and back is enough for a day trip.
- **9:30** Tofuku-ji — stunning Zen garden complex, less crowded than Kinkaku-ji.
- **11:00** Ninenzaka / Sannenzaka — stone-paved lanes through preserved Edo-era buildings. Browse shops; try matcha soft-serve.

**Summer note:** Be at Fushimi Inari by 8 AM at the latest — by 10 AM the heat on the unshaded mountain path is intense.

---

### Phase 2 — Midday (12:00–14:00): Lunch + Shade Break

- **12:00** Lunch in Gion or Higashiyama — kaiseki set lunch (cheaper than dinner), soba, or tofu cuisine. Many spots open at 11:30; lines form fast.
- **13:00** Yasaka Shrine — free entry, open grounds, good for a shaded stroll.
- **Optional:** Nishiki Market (10-min taxi/bus) — covered arcade, ideal midday escape from sun.

---

### Phase 3 — Afternoon (14:00–17:00): Northwestern Arashiyama

Take the Hankyu line or a bus to Arashiyama (~30 min).

- **14:30** Bamboo Grove — brief but iconic; visit midweek if possible to avoid weekend crowds.
- **15:00** Tenryu-ji garden (UNESCO) — moss garden and pond with Arashiyama mountains as borrowed scenery.
- **16:00** Walk along Togetsukyo Bridge and riverside — cooler near the water; rent a rowboat if you like.

### Phase 4 — Evening (17:30–21:00): Gion & Illuminations
