# One-Day Kyoto Itinerary (Summer)

Here's a special itinerary that balances iconic sites with a few less-crowded gems, paced for summer heat.

---

## Morning (Start early — before 9 AM)

**Fushimi Inari Taisha** (free, open 24 hours)
Walk the lower torii gates before the crowds arrive. In summer, aim to finish this by 8:30 AM before temperatures peak. The full hike to the summit takes 2–3 hours; even 45 minutes up gives the iconic tunnel views.

**Tofuku-ji** (¥500 garden fee)
A 10-minute walk/bus from Fushimi Inari. The Zen garden and bridge views are stunning and far less visited than comparable spots.

---

## Midday (11 AM – 2 PM)

**Nishiki Market** ("Kyoto's Kitchen")
Browse the covered arcade for food stalls — skewered tofu, pickles, tamagoyaki. A good way to eat lunch incrementally while staying cool.

**Nijo Castle** (¥1,000)
The "nightingale floors" and painted Edo-period interiors are worth the heat. Check hours — it closes on certain Tuesdays.

---

## Afternoon (2 PM – 5 PM, hottest part of the day)

**Kyoto National Museum or Kyoto International Manga Museum**
Take refuge indoors during peak heat. The National Museum holds rotating exhibitions of national treasures.

Alternatively: rest at your accommodation for an hour — this is common practice in Japan during summer afternoons.

**Philosopher's Path** (free)
Walk the canal-side path from Nanzen-ji toward Ginkaku-ji (Silver Pavilion) in the late afternoon once it cools slightly. The path is shaded and pleasant.

---

## Evening (5 PM onward)

**Gion District**
Wander Hanamikoji Street at dusk. If you're lucky you may spot a geiko or maiko heading to an engagement (most active 6–8 PM). No reservation needed to walk; just be respectful near ochaya.

**Dinner in Gion or Pontocho**
Pontocho alley has riverside (kawayuka) dining platforms over the Kamo River — a quintessential summer Kyoto experience.

> **Reserve in advance:** Pontocho kawayuka restaurants fill up weeks ahead in summer. Book as soon as your dates are confirmed.

---

## Things to Verify in Advance

| Item | What to Check |
|------|--------------|
| **Nijo Castle** | Closed some Tuesdays; confirm the specific dates for your week |
| **Tofuku-ji** | Special seasonal closures possible; verify garden hours |
| **Pontocho kawayuka restaurants** | Reservations often required; platforms run roughly May–September |
| **Gion Matsuri** | Held throughout July — if you're visiting in July, the city will be packed and some roads closed; adjust routing accordingly |
| **Museum exhibitions** | Check the Kyoto National Museum website for what's on during your specific week |
| **Train/bus IC card** | Load a Suica or ICOCA card for easy transit — most buses and trains accept it |

---

**A note on summer heat:** Kyoto in summer (especially July–August) is extremely humid and can exceed 35°C. Carry a hand towel, stay hydrated, and don't underestimate the midday sun — the indoor breaks in this itinerary are intentional, not optional.
