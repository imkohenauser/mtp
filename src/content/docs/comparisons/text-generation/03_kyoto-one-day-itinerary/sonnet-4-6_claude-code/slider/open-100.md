A week in Kyoto in summer — there's so much that could make a "special" one-day itinerary, and it might be worth pausing on what *special* means to you before settling on a route.

---

**One possibility: a classic temples-and-neighborhoods arc**

- Morning (early): Fushimi Inari Taisha — the torii gates thin out dramatically before 8am; in summer heat this timing matters a lot
- Mid-morning: Nishiki Market for a quick walk and something light to eat
- Lunch: somewhere in Gion or Higashiyama
- Afternoon: Philosopher's Path → Nanzen-ji → Eikan-do (the garden ponds are lovely)
- Late afternoon: Kinkaku-ji if you haven't already, though it's almost always crowded
- Evening: back to Gion for *maiko* sightings around dusk

But another frame entirely might be: **one neighborhood, deeply** rather than crossing the city. Arashiyama in the morning — bamboo grove, Tenryu-ji garden, a boat on the Oi River — could fill a whole day without feeling rushed.

---

**Things worth verifying in advance**

- Tenryu-ji's inner garden requires a separate ticket; that entrance can sell out
- Some traditional restaurants (kaiseki style especially) require reservations weeks ahead — summer is peak domestic travel season
- A few temples close specific buildings on rotating days; checking the temple's official site for the exact dates of your visit is worth it
- The Koto-in sub-temple at Daitoku-ji sometimes requires advance reservation periods
- Arashiyama's *sagano* scenic railway is popular and can be booked up

---

One thing to consider: summer in Kyoto is genuinely hot and humid — the itinerary that looks perfect on paper can feel brutal if it involves walking exposed streets between 11am and 3pm. Some people build a mid-afternoon rest into the plan intentionally.

What draws you most — traditional culture and temples, food and neighborhoods, nature and gardens, or something else? That might point toward which shape of day would feel most memorable to you.
