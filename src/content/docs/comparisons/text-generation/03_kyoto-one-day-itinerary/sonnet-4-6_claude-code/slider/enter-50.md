## One-Day Special Kyoto Itinerary — Summer

**Assumption:** You have a full day (roughly 8:00–20:00), comfortable walking shoes, and are staying somewhere central. Summer in Kyoto means high heat and humidity, so the itinerary front-loads outdoor sites in the early morning and moves indoors or into shade during midday.

---

### Morning (8:00–12:00) — Eastern Higashiyama

**8:00 — Fushimi Inari Taisha**
Start here before the crowds. The lower shrine area and the first gate tunnels (senbon torii) are most iconic. A full hike to the summit takes ~2 hours; heading up 30–40 minutes and turning back is enough for the atmosphere.
- *Verify:* Open 24 hours, no reservation needed. Arrive by 8:00 to beat tour groups.

**10:00 — Kiyomizudera**
Head north to Higashiyama. The wooden stage over the hillside is the classic summer view. The approach streets (Ninenzaka, Sannenzaka) are lined with shops and cafés — good for a quick breakfast or matcha ice cream.
- *Verify:* Opens 6:00, closes 18:00 (extended hours some summer evenings for special illuminations — check the official site). Admission ~500 yen.

---

### Midday (12:00–15:00) — Shaded lunch and Nishiki Market

**12:00 — Lunch near Gion**
Gion has mid-range kaiseki lunch sets and tofu cuisine restaurants. Alternatively, head to Nishiki Market (covered indoor arcade) for street food stalls — pickles, grilled skewers, tamagoyaki.
- *Verify:* Popular restaurants fill quickly. If you want a sit-down kaiseki lunch, reserve at least a week ahead.

**13:30 — Nishiki Market & Teramachi**
A short indoor walk to cool down. Browse the market, then the Teramachi covered shopping arcade.
- *No reservation needed.*

---

### Afternoon (15:00–18:00) — Arashiyama

**15:00 — Arashiyama Bamboo Grove & Tenryuji**
Take the city bus or Hankyu/Sagano line out to Arashiyama. The bamboo grove path is a 5-minute walk but very atmospheric. Tenryuji garden next door is a UNESCO site with a pond garden and mountain backdrop.
- *Verify:* Tenryuji garden opens 8:30–17:30 (17:00 last entry in summer). Admission ~500–1,000 yen depending on which area. No reservation needed for the garden, but the interior of the main hall requires a separate ticket.

**16:30 — Togetsukyo Bridge & riverside**
A 10-minute walk to the iconic bridge over the Oi River. Good for photos in the late-afternoon light. Riverbank cafés here are a good place to rest before heading back.

---

### Evening (18:00–20:00) — Gion & Pontocho

**18:00 — Gion walk**
Stroll Hanamikoji Street at dusk. This is the most likely time to spot maiko (apprentice geisha) traveling between engagements — respectful, quiet observation only.

**19:00 — Dinner in Pontocho**
The narrow lantern-lit alley along the Kamogawa River. Many restaurants offer kawadoko (riverside terrace dining), a Kyoto summer tradition.
- *Verify:* Kawadoko terraces are weather-dependent and fill quickly. Reserve 2–4 weeks in advance for summer evenings, especially weekends.
