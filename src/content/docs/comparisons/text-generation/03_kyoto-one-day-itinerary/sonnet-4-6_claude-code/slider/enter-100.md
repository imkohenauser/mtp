## One-Day Itinerary

### Phase 1 — Morning (8:00–12:00): Eastern Kyoto (Higashiyama)

Start early to beat the heat and the crowds.

**8:00 — Fushimi Inari Taisha**
Walk the lower torii tunnel before tour buses arrive. The full hike to the summit (Yotsutsuji viewpoint) takes ~2 hours; the lower section alone is 30–40 min and spectacular. Free, always open.

**10:00 — Travel north to Higashiyama**
Take the JR Nara Line to Tofukuji, then walk or bus to Kiyomizudera (or taxi: ~15 min).

**10:30 — Kiyomizudera**
The wooden stage view over Kyoto is iconic. After descending, walk the Sannen-zaka / Ninen-zaka stone-paved lanes to Yasaka Pagoda — the most photogenic stretch in the city.

**12:00 — Lunch at Gion**
Several kaiseki lunch sets and tofu-cuisine restaurants open from noon. Reservations strongly recommended (see below).

---

### Phase 2 — Afternoon (13:30–17:30): Central & Northern Kyoto

**13:30 — Nishiki Market ("Kyoto's Kitchen")**
A covered street market for snacks (tamagoyaki, pickles, dango). Short walk from Gion via Shijo.

**14:30 — Nijo Castle**
UNESCO World Heritage, famous for its "nightingale floors." Inner palace has excellent English signage. Allow 1.5 hours.

**16:30 — Arashiyama (optional swap)**
If you'd prefer nature over a castle, ride the JR Sagano Line west to Arashiyama instead: Bamboo Grove + Tenryuji garden in an afternoon. Pick one or the other — doing both is rushed.

---

### Phase 3 — Evening (18:00–21:00): Gion & Dinner

**18:00 — Gion district stroll**
Hanamikoji Street in early evening has the best chance of spotting a geiko or maiko heading to engagements. Walk quietly and respectfully; photography rules apply.

**19:00 — Dinner**
Options:
- **Kaiseki** (traditional multi-course): requires advance reservation weeks out in summer
- **Yakitori / izakaya** near Pontocho Alley: atmospheric, walk-in friendly
- **Ramen** at one of Kyoto's gyoza/ramen shops: no reservation needed

**20:30 — Optional: Kamo River evening walk**
Locals sit along the riverbanks in summer evenings (the famous "bed seating" _yuka_ restaurants are on the opposite bank, Pontocho side). A pleasant end to the day.
