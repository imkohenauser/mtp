## One-Day Kyoto Itinerary — Summer (Special Edition)

### 5:30 AM — Fushimi Inari Taisha
Arrive at dawn. Walk as far up the mountain as time allows; the upper trails are far less crowded than the famous lower gates and offer views over the city. Carry water. The shrine is always open; no admission fee.

*Why this first:* The orange torii tunnels in early-morning mist or golden light are genuinely otherworldly. By 9 AM the lower section is elbow-to-elbow with tour groups.

---

### 7:30 AM — Breakfast
Return toward the city. Options: a traditional kissaten (old-school coffee shop) near Tofukuji, or a konbini breakfast if you want to move fast.

---

### 9:00 AM — Kiyomizudera + Ninenzaka / Sannenzaka
Arrive at Kiyomizudera early. The wooden stage over the hillside is striking; the Otowa waterfall below has three streams (tradition holds you choose one to drink from). Descend via the stone-paved Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka lanes — lined with machiya townhouses and small shops. Unhurried, this is 1.5–2 hours.

---

### 11:30 AM — Lunch in Gion
Head toward Gion for lunch. The area around Hanamikoji has tofu kaiseki sets and bento lunch menus at prices far lower than dinner. Alternatively, Nishiki Market (covered arcade) is a 10-minute walk further west and good for grazing on street food in shade.

---

### 13:30 PM — Philosopher's Path → Nanzenji
Walk the Tetsugaku-no-Michi (Philosopher's Path), a canal-side stone path lined with trees. It's genuinely shaded and relatively quiet in the afternoon. At the northern end, enter Nanzenji — the massive Sanmon gate and the red-brick aqueduct running through the temple grounds are unexpectedly photogenic. Sub-temple Tenjuan has a mossy rock garden and koi pond worth 20 minutes.

*Swap option if heat is extreme:* Replace this slot with Nijo Castle (Nijo-jo) — the tatami-floored palace interior is cool and the "nightingale floors" are genuinely remarkable.

---

### 16:30 PM — Return to hotel / freshen up
This is important in summer. Kyoto humidity makes late afternoon genuinely draining.

---

### 18:00 PM — Kawayuka dinner, Pontocho
Pontocho is a narrow lantern-lit alley running parallel to the Kamo River. In summer (roughly May–September), restaurants extend platforms (kawayuka) over the river — you dine outdoors above the water with a breeze, watching the city lights. It's one of the most distinctively Kyoto summer experiences there is. Budget ¥6,000–¥15,000+ per person depending on restaurant tier.

---

### 20:00 PM — Evening walk, Gion Hanamikoji
A 10-minute walk from Pontocho. The main street of Gion is at its best after dark — lanterns lit, occasional glimpse of geiko or maiko heading to appointments (most likely to spot them 17:30–18:30 or after 21:00; avoid blocking their path or using flash). Yasaka Shrine at the end of the street is illuminated and open late.
