## Special One-Day Kyoto Itinerary (Summer)

### Morning — Higashiyama (6:30–10:00)

**6:30 — Fushimi Inari Taisha**
Walk the lower torii gates before the crowds arrive. In summer, the forested path is cooler at dawn. The full mountain hike (~2 hrs) is optional; the photogenic lower section takes 30–45 min.

**8:30 — Breakfast at Nishiki Market area or a kissaten near Gion**
Grab a light meal; the market itself opens around 9–10.

**9:30 — Ninen-zaka & Sannen-zaka lanes → Kiyomizudera**
The stone-paved alleys are manageable before noon. Kiyomizudera's main stage (the suspended wooden platform with forest views) is the anchor. ⚠️ *Timed-entry tickets for the inner sanctuary (Okuno-in) — verify availability and hours before your trip.*

---

### Midday — Rest & Lunch (11:30–13:30)

**Gion or Shijo area**
Seek a restaurant with air conditioning. Options: kaiseki lunch sets (lighter on the wallet at lunch than dinner), or tofu cuisine (yudofu) at a riverside restaurant on the Kamo River.

---

### Afternoon — Philosopher's Path → Nanzenji (13:30–16:00)

**Philosopher's Path (Tetsugaku no Michi)**
A canal-side walking path (~2 km) connecting Ginkakuji (Silver Pavilion) to Nanzenji. Shaded, quiet in the afternoon.

**Ginkakuji (Silver Pavilion)**
Zen garden and sand cone. ⚠️ *Timed-entry system in place during peak season — check the official site; advance tickets are recommended.*

**Nanzenji**
Walk through the aqueduct (Suirokaku) and the main gate (Sanmon). Free to wander the grounds; sub-temples like Tenjuan charge separate entry.

---

### Evening — Arashiyama (16:30–21:00)

Take the subway/bus west (~40 min).

**17:00 — Bamboo Grove & Okochi Sanso Garden**
The bamboo grove is a 5-minute walk but always crowded; go for the experience. Okochi Sanso (the villa garden of a silent-film actor) is an underrated gem — entry includes matcha and a sweet. ⚠️ *Closes at 17:00; adjust timing or skip to the garden on a different visit if arriving late.*

**18:30 — Dinner in Arashiyama**
Riverside restaurants along the Oi River. Tofu and yuba dishes are regional specialties. On clear evenings the view of Arashiyama mountain at dusk is excellent.

**20:00 — Togetsukyo Bridge at night**
A short walk from most restaurants. The lit bridge over the river is a calm, crowd-free end to the day.
