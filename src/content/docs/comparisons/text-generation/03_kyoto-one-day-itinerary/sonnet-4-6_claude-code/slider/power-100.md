## One Day in Kyoto — Summer Special

### Morning: Fushimi Inari (6:00–8:30 AM)

**Start here, arrive at dawn.** The thousands of torii gates at Fushimi Inari are genuinely stunning — and in summer, genuinely brutal by 10 AM. Beat the heat and the crowds by arriving at opening light. Climb to the first summit (Yotsutsuji intersection) in about 30 minutes. The view over Kyoto justifies every step.

- No reservation needed, open 24 hours.
- Walk back down by 8:30, eat breakfast at a stall near the entrance.

---

### Late Morning: Nishiki Market & Downtown (9:30–11:30 AM)

**Eat before the crowds arrive.** Nishiki Market is a covered shopping street with pickled vegetables, grilled skewers, tofu, and tamago. It is the kitchen of Kyoto. Walk it end to end, eat as you go.

- Most stalls open around 9:00–9:30 AM.
- Closes around 6:00 PM.

---

### Afternoon: Arashiyama (12:30–4:00 PM)

**The bamboo grove, the river, the temple — do all three.**

1. **Tenryu-ji** — a top-tier Zen garden with a pond that mirrors the Arashiyama mountains. This is the crown jewel of Kyoto's garden tradition. **Reservation not required but arrive early in the afternoon.**
   - Hours: 8:30 AM–5:30 PM (last entry 5:00 PM). Verify for summer 2026.
2. **Arashiyama Bamboo Grove** — walk through immediately after Tenryu-ji since it is directly adjacent.
3. **Togetsukyo Bridge** — five-minute walk to the river. Buy cold matcha soft serve. Stand on the bridge.

- Tenryu-ji has a garden-only ticket and a full interior ticket. **Get the interior ticket.** Worth the extra cost.

---

### Late Afternoon: Philosopher's Path & Ginkaku-ji (4:30–6:30 PM)

**The light at dusk on this canal is unrepeatable.** The Philosopher's Path is a stone walkway along a canal lined with cherry trees (bare in summer, but still beautiful). Walk north to Ginkaku-ji (the Silver Pavilion).

- Ginkaku-ji hours: 8:30 AM–5:00 PM. **Confirm closing time** — you must arrive by 5:30 PM at the latest.
- The moss garden inside Ginkaku-ji is among the finest in Japan.

---

### Evening: Gion & Dinner (7:00 PM onwards)

**End here.** Walk the stone-paved lanes of Gion (Hanamikoji Street in particular) at dusk. If it is summer, you may encounter a maiko or geiko heading to an engagement — this is real, not staged.

For dinner:

- **kaiseki**: book 2–4 weeks in advance; top restaurants fill up fast in summer
- **tofu cuisine (yudofu)**: easier to walk in, quintessentially Kyoto
- **izakaya near Gion**: no reservation required, local atmosphere

---

## What to Verify Before You Go

| Item | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Ginkaku-ji closing time** | 5:00 PM is standard, but seasonal variations occur |
| **Tenryu-ji interior access** | Confirm it is open (occasional private events close the main hall) |
| **Kaiseki dinner reservation** | Book 2–4 weeks ahead minimum for summer travel |
| **Heat forecast** | July–August Kyoto hits 35°C+. Midday at outdoor sites is punishing — structure your day to be indoors or in shade from 11 AM–3 PM |
| **Gion Matsuri dates** | If your week overlaps with Gion Matsuri (July 1–31, peak July 17 and 24), downtown crowds are massive. Either lean into the festival or plan around it |

---

**The non-negotiable:** if Gion Matsuri overlaps your stay, watch the Yamaboko Junko parade on July 17 or July 24. It happens once a year and it is extraordinary. Reserve a viewing spot in advance — hotels and travel agencies sell bleacher seats, and they sell out.
