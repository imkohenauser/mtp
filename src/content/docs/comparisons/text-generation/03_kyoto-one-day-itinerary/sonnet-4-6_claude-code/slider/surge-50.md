**One Day in Kyoto — A Special Itinerary**

---

**Morning: Arashiyama**

Start early. 7:00 AM. Beat the crowds.

- **Tenryu-ji Garden** (opens 8:30 AM) — UNESCO World Heritage zen garden, moss and raked gravel, a pond that hasn't changed in 700 years
- **Bamboo Grove** — walk it immediately after Tenryu-ji before tour groups arrive; late morning it becomes a wall of selfie sticks
- **Okochi Sanso Villa** — hidden gem, tea and sweets included in admission, panoramic views over the whole Arashiyama valley

> **Verify:** Tenryu-ji inner garden has separate admission from the bamboo path. Confirm hours at [tenryuji.com](https://www.tenryuji.com/en/).

---

**Late Morning: Nishiki Market → Nijo Castle**

Taxi or bus east into central Kyoto.

- **Nishiki Market** ("Kyoto's Kitchen") — 400-year-old covered arcade, 100+ stalls, pickled plum, grilled skewers, tofu donuts. Open ~10 AM most vendors.
- **Nijo Castle** (closes 5 PM, no entry after 4 PM) — Edo-period shogun's palace, "nightingale floors" that chirp underfoot to detect assassins. Budget 1.5 hours minimum.

> **Verify:** Nijo Castle closes entirely on certain Tuesdays and during early January. Check [city.kyoto.lg.jp](https://nijo-jocastle.city.kyoto.lg.jp/en/) before going.

---

**Afternoon: Fushimi Inari + Gion**

- **Fushimi Inari Taisha** — the 10,000 torii gate mountain shrine. Open 24 hours, free. Go by 2 PM; the full hike is 2–3 hours. The upper trails thin out. Do them.
- **Gion district** — walk Hanamikoji Street after returning from Inari, ~5 PM. Wooden machiya townhouses, lanterns starting to glow, occasional maiko sighting.

> **Verify:** Fushimi Inari has no admission but trail conditions matter in summer heat — carry water, wear light clothing. The mountain is exposed.

---

**Evening: Kaiseki Dinner**

Book. In. Advance. This is non-negotiable.

Kyoto kaiseki (multi-course traditional cuisine) at a proper restaurant requires reservations weeks ahead in summer, especially for English-friendly seats. Options to research:

- **Kikunoi Honten** — award-winning, English menus available
- **Mizai** — intimate, seasonal, reservation-only
- **Tankuma Kitamise** — centuries-old establishment

> **Verify:** Contact restaurants 4–6 weeks before your visit. Many require a credit card hold. Some don't accept same-week bookings at all.

---

**What to Verify Before You Go**

| Item | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Tenryu-ji inner garden hours | Summer hours sometimes shift |
| Nijo Castle closure days | Irregular Tuesday closures |
| Kaiseki reservation | Fills up fast in peak summer |
| Bus IC card (ICOCA/Suica) | Most efficient way to move around; get it at Kyoto Station |
| Weather forecast | July–August hits 35°C+ with humidity; plan shade breaks |

---

Summer Kyoto is hot, crowded, and completely worth it. The key is the 7 AM start — that alone separates your day from everyone else's.
