---

### Morning

**6:30 AM — Fushimi Inari-taisha**
Start at the iconic vermillion torii gates before the heat and crowds peak. Climb as far as Yotsuji intersection (about 30 min up) for a city view and a quieter experience. The shrine is open 24 hours — no reservation needed.

**8:30 AM — Nishiki Market**
Head north for breakfast at Kyoto's "kitchen." Try dashimaki tamago (rolled omelette) or yudofu. Most stalls open around 9–10 AM, so arrive early and browse the outer edges first.

---

### Late Morning

**10:00 AM — Nijo Castle**
A UNESCO World Heritage site with "nightingale floors" that creak as a security measure — an experience unique to this castle. Crowds are manageable before noon.

> **Verify:** Seasonal closures (typically December, Tuesdays in Jan/Feb/Jul/Aug). In summer 2026, confirm current hours at the official Nijo Castle site.

### Afternoon

**12:30 PM — Lunch in Gion**
Try kaiseki-lite at a machiya (townhouse) restaurant, or a simpler bowl of Kyoto-style ramen. Gion's side streets have mid-range options without long waits.

**2:00 PM — Kennin-ji Temple**
Kyoto's oldest Zen temple — compact, less crowded than Kinkaku-ji, and visually stunning with its dragon ceiling painting. Opens 10 AM, closes 5 PM (last entry 4:30 PM).

**3:30 PM — Hanamikoji Street & Gion Stroll**
Walk the preserved machiya streetscape. Late afternoon light is beautiful here. Geiko/maiko sightings are more common around 5–6 PM as they head to evening engagements — observe respectfully from the sidewalk, no photos without consent.

---

### Evening

**5:30 PM — Philosopher's Path (Brief Walk)**
A 2 km canal-side path connecting Ginkaku-ji area to Nanzen-ji — peaceful in the long summer evening light. Skip the full length if fatigued; even a 20-minute walk is rewarding.

**7:00 PM — Dinner: Pontocho Alley**
Narrow lantern-lit street lined with restaurants, many with *kawayuka* (riverside terraces over the Kamo River) — a Kyoto summer tradition. Reservations are strongly recommended for terrace seating.

> **Verify:** Kawayuka season typically runs May–September; confirm 2026 dates with your chosen restaurant. Book 1–3 weeks in advance for popular spots.

**9:00 PM — Evening at Yasaka Shrine**
Free, open at night, beautifully lit. A calm end to the day in the heart of Gion.
