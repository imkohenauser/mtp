## Kyoto Special One-Day Summer Itinerary

This itinerary is built around a single throughline: **the contrast between Kyoto's sacred mountain north and its refined merchant south**, experienced in a single day. Rather than cramming in landmarks, every stop deepens that one theme.

---

### The Route: Fushimi Inari → Nishiki → Gion → Philosopher's Path → Kurama

| Time | Stop | Why it fits |
|------|------|-------------|
| 06:00 | **Fushimi Inari Taisha** – walk the inner torii tunnel to Yotsutsuji | The lower mountain is already crowded by 8 AM; 6 AM gives you the tunnel nearly alone |
| 08:30 | **Nishiki Market** ("Kyoto's kitchen") | Opens at 09:00 for most stalls; arrive as shutters go up, buy breakfast from stalls selling tofu skewers and tamagoyaki |
| 10:30 | **Gion Shimbashi** (cobblestone canal street) | The machiya townhouses are best photographed in morning light before tour buses arrive |
| 12:00 | Lunch at a kaiseki lunch set in Gion (cheaper than dinner; many restaurants offer ¥3,000–5,000 midday sets vs. ¥15,000+ dinner) | |
| 14:00 | **Philosopher's Path** (哲学の道) – walk north from Nanzenji to Ginkakuji | 2 km canal-side walk shaded by trees; in summer, the canal is low and clear |
| 15:30 | **Ginkakuji (Silver Pavilion)** | Last major stop before heading to the mountains |
| 17:00 | Train to **Kurama** (Eizan Railway from Demachiyanagi, ~30 min) | Kurama-dera on the mountain feels completely different from the city – dense cedar forest, cooler air, cedar smell |
| 18:30 | Soak at **Kurama Onsen** (outdoor bath on the mountain, open until 21:00) | The open-air bath faces the cedar forest; summer evenings here are the practical payoff of the day |
| 20:00 | Return to Kyoto city center |

---

### What to Verify in Advance

| Item | What to check | Why it matters |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **Fushimi Inari** | No reservation needed, but confirm no nighttime access restrictions have changed | Occasionally closed for festivals (e.g., Motomiya-sai in late July) |
| **Nishiki Market** | Individual stall hours vary; some open 09:00, others 10:00 | If you arrive at 08:30, several stalls will still be closed |
| **Gion kaiseki lunch** | **Reservation required.** Book at least 2–3 weeks ahead for popular spots (Kikunoi Roan, Nakamuraya) | Walk-ins are rarely accepted; summer is high season |
| **Ginkakuji** | Confirm opening hours (typically 08:30–17:00 in summer, extended to 20:00 for some periods) | Crowded; arriving by 15:30 means lines are shorter than midday |

---

**Single most important advance action:** Reserve the kaiseki lunch. Everything else on this itinerary is walk-in, but without a lunch reservation in Gion in summer, you will spend the best daylight hours waiting for a table or settling for a restaurant you didn't choose.
