## A Special One-Day Itinerary in Kyoto (Summer)

### Morning

**Fushimi Inari Taisha — first light**
Start before 7 a.m. if you can manage it. The thousands of vermilion torii gates on Mount Inari are genuinely magical in the early haze, and the crowds that arrive by mid-morning simply don't exist yet. The full hike to the summit and back is about 4 km; even just going as far as Yotsuji junction (about halfway) rewards you with a lookout over the city. There are no opening hours — the shrine never closes.

**Nishiki Market for breakfast**
After coming back down, head into central Kyoto and wander Nishiki Market. This narrow covered arcade is Kyoto's kitchen: vendors selling fresh tofu, pickled vegetables, grilled skewers, and tamagoyaki (rolled omelette). It opens around 9–10 a.m. depending on the shop. By the way, the stall selling dashi-rolled tamago on a stick near the Teramachi end has been a quiet local favorite for decades — the kind of thing that doesn't appear on most lists.

### Midday

**Nijo Castle**
Worth the entry fee (¥1,300) for the "nightingale floors" alone — the floorboards in the palace squeak deliberately to detect intruders, a genuine 17th-century security system. In summer, the castle garden's lotus pond sometimes blooms spectacularly. Opens at 8:45 a.m., last entry 4 p.m. Closed Tuesdays.

**Lunch near Nishijin**
The Nishijin weaving district, just north, has a cluster of old townhouse restaurants. Yudofu (tofu simmered in kombu broth) is a classic Kyoto lunch that's both light and restorative in the heat.

### Afternoon

**Ryoanji's rock garden, then Kinkakuji**
These two sit close together and are usually done in the wrong order by visitors — Kinkakuji first because it's famous. Go to Ryoanji first instead. The dry rock garden is best with some quiet around it, and you'll have more of that in the early afternoon. Then walk or take the bus to Kinkakuji (the Golden Pavilion) for the late afternoon light on the water. Both are open from around 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.

A small digression: Ryoanji also has a teahouse, Sekkatei, that serves matcha and yudofu in a garden setting. It's easy to miss. Worth knowing about if you want a moment to slow down.

### Evening

**Philosopher's Path toward Nanzenji**
As the heat softens, walk the Philosopher's Path — a canal-side stone path lined with cherry trees (bare in summer, but still beautiful in a quieter way). Follow it south toward Nanzenji. The aqueduct there, a Roman-style brick structure from the 1890s, sits inside the temple grounds and surprises almost everyone who hasn't seen it before.

**Dinner in Gion**
End in Gion, Kyoto's geisha district. Hanamikoji Street is beautiful at dusk. For dinner, the side streets off the main lanes have kaiseki restaurants at a range of price points. In summer, many places set up *kawayuka* — riverside platforms built over the Kamo River — which is a distinctly Kyoto experience. These are popular; reservations help.
