## Morning (7:00–10:30)

**Fushimi Inari Taisha** — Start here at 7 AM. The thousands of torii gates are best before tour buses arrive (~9 AM). The full hike to the summit takes 2–3 hours; even a 45-minute walk to Yotsutsuji overlook is rewarding. The shrine is open 24 hours with no admission fee. No reservation needed, but verify the JR Inari station schedule beforehand.

---

## Mid-Morning (10:30–12:30)

**Nishiki Market** — Take JR/Keihan to central Kyoto. Walk Nishiki-koji for a late breakfast or snacks (grilled tofu, pickles, matcha). Most stalls open by 10 AM; the covered arcade is naturally cool. Hours are roughly 9 AM–6 PM, but individual shops vary — confirm your top targets in advance.

---

## Afternoon (13:00–16:00)

**Gion & Yasaka Shrine** — Walk east to Gion's Hanamikoji street and Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka lanes. These are open-air and free. For Yasaka Shrine itself, no reservation needed. **Summer priority:** the Gion Matsuri festival runs through most of July — if your dates overlap, this transforms the entire neighborhood. Check the festival calendar.

---

## Late Afternoon (16:30–18:30)

**Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)** — Budget about 45 minutes. Admission is ¥500. Open 9 AM–5 PM; arrive by 4:30 PM at the latest. The low-angle afternoon light on the gold leaf is exceptional. Tickets are sold at the gate; no advance booking required, but summer is peak season and entry queues can run 20–30 minutes.

---

## Evening (19:00+)

**Pontocho or Gion dinner** — Return downtown for dinner. Pontocho alley has riverside terraces (yuka dining) that open in summer — a Kyoto-specific experience worth booking 1–2 weeks ahead.

---

## What to verify before you go

| Item | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Gion Matsuri dates | Festival (mid-to-late July) reshapes the entire area and traffic |
| Yuka (riverside terrace) reservation | Fills up fast; book through restaurant websites or a concierge |
| Kinkaku-ji queue times | Arrive early or late in the afternoon to cut wait |
| JR Nara/Inari train schedule | Confirm first/last train times if you're staying farther from the city center |
| Heat index forecast | Kyoto summers are genuinely dangerous (35°C+, high humidity) — carry water, plan indoor breaks |

---

**One practical note on summer heat:** schedule the outdoor walking segments before 11 AM and after 4 PM. Midday (12–4 PM) is best spent indoors — Nishiki Market, a museum, or a machiya café with AC.
