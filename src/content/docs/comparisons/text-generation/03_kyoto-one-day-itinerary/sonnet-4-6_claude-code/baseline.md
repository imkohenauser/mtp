# Kyoto One-Day Summer Itinerary

Summer in Kyoto is hot and humid (July–August can hit 35°C+), so the plan below front-loads outdoor sites in the cool morning, retreats indoors midday, and finishes with an evening stroll.

---

## Morning (6:30 – 11:00)

**6:30 — Fushimi Inari Taisha**
Walk the lower torii gates before the crowds arrive. You don't need to hike to the summit — the first 30–45 minutes up is the iconic stretch.
- Free, open 24 hours — no reservation needed.

**9:00 — Nishiki Market ("Kyoto's Kitchen")**
Narrow covered market for breakfast snacks: tamagoyaki, pickled vegetables, fresh tofu. Most stalls open around 9–10 AM.
- Most stalls close by 6 PM; a few close on Wednesday or Thursday. Verify individual vendor hours.

---

## Midday (11:00 – 15:00)

**11:00 — Nijo Castle**
A UNESCO World Heritage Site with "nightingale floors" that creak to detect intruders. The interiors are air-conditioned in parts.
- Hours: 8:45 AM – 5:00 PM (last entry 4:00 PM). **Closed Tuesdays** (and some Tuesdays in July/December). Verify the summer schedule on the official site.
- Admission: ~¥1,300.

**13:00 — Lunch: Kaiseki or Obanzai**
The Kawaramachi/Gion area has many mid-range obanzai (Kyoto home-style) restaurants. For kaiseki, reservations are often required weeks in advance — book before your trip.

---

## Afternoon (15:00 – 18:00)

**15:00 — Arashiyama**
Take the train west (Hankyu or JR San-in line). Visit in this order:
1. **Tenryu-ji garden** — a Zen garden with a pond; the bamboo grove is right behind it. Hours: 8:30 AM – 5:30 PM (extended in summer). Entry: ~¥500 (garden only).
2. **Bamboo Grove** — free and public, but crowded by mid-morning; by 3 PM it's more manageable.
3. **Togetsukyo Bridge** — a short walk; nice for photos by the river.

---

## Evening (18:00 – 21:00)

**18:00 — Gion District**
Stroll Hanamikoji Street for the chance to spot geiko or maiko heading to evening engagements (most likely 5:30–7:00 PM). Respectful observation only.

**19:00 — Dinner in Gion or Pontocho**
Pontocho alley has riverside terrace dining (*kawayuka*), a summer-only Kyoto tradition. Many restaurants fill up — **book in advance**, especially on weekends.

**20:30 — Yasaka Shrine**
Lit up at night and free to enter. Especially atmospheric in July during Gion Matsuri (the entire month of July is festival season — check the specific dates, as some events close roads or draw very large crowds).

---

## Key Things to Verify in Advance

| Item | Why |
|---|---|
| Nijo Castle closure days | Closed some Tuesdays; schedule varies by month |
| Kaiseki dinner reservation | Top restaurants book 1–4 weeks out |
| Tenryu-ji hours | Slightly extended in summer, verify exact dates |
| Kawayuka (riverside dining) | Typically May–September, but dates vary by restaurant |
| Gion Matsuri dates | If visiting July, road closures and crowds can affect the plan |
| Heat/hydration | Carry water; summer heat can be dangerous — build in rest breaks |

---

This itinerary keeps transit simple (Fushimi → central Kyoto → Arashiyama → Gion) and balances iconic landmarks with quieter moments. Let me know if you want to swap anything in — temples, tea ceremony experiences, day trips to Nara, etc.
