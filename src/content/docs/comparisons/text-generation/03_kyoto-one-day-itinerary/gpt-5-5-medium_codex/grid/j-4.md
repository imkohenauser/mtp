**Best One-Day Plan: Kurama To Kibune, Then Kyoto By Lantern Light**

Make this your “special” day: northern Kyoto in the morning, riverside dining in the heat of the day, and old Kyoto at dusk. It is more memorable than stacking famous temples, and it uses summer as an advantage instead of fighting it.

**Itinerary**

Source: [Kurama-dera official site](https://www.kuramadera.or.jp/)

Have **kawadoko lunch in Kibune**, seated on platforms over the river. This is the anchor of the day. Book this in advance, especially for weekends, August, or Obon. Check Kurama-dera hours and cable car status the week of your visit; mountain access and cable schedules can change.

Check weather. If heavy rain or thunder is forecast, skip the Kurama-to-Kibune hike and go directly to Kibune by train/bus for shrine plus lunch.

Avoid Saturday, Sunday, and Obon if possible. A weekday will be materially better.

Bring cash, water, insect repellent, and a light layer. Kibune is cooler than central Kyoto, but summer humidity still wins if you underprepare.
