English began as **Old English** (Anglo-Saxon), brought to Britain by Germanic tribes — Angles, Saxons, and Jutes — around the 5th century AD. It was heavily inflected, with complex noun cases, and looks almost unrecognizable today (*Hwæt! We Gardena…*).

The result is a language with a Germanic skeleton, a Romance vocabulary layer, and a chaotic spelling system — uniquely positioned as today's global lingua franca precisely because of its hybrid, borrowing nature.
