### 1. Old English (c. 450–1100 AD)

*   **Linguistic Shift:** English adopted thousands of French and Latin words, particularly in fields like politics, law, and art (e.g., *court, judge, beef* vs. Germanic *cow*).

### 3. Early Modern English (c. 1500–1800)
This period saw the **Great Vowel Shift**, a massive change in pronunciation that fundamentally altered how English sounded. The invention of the printing press by William Caxton helped standardize spelling and grammar.
*   **Key Influence:** The Renaissance introduced many Greek and Latin loanwords. The works of **William Shakespeare** and the publication of the **King James Bible** had a profound impact on the language's development.

### 4. Late Modern English (c. 1800–Present)
The primary difference between Early and Late Modern English is vocabulary. The **Industrial Revolution** and the expansion of the **British Empire** required new terms for technology and brought English into contact with cultures worldwide.
*   **Global Expansion:** English adopted words from hundreds of other languages (e.g., *shampoo* from Hindi, *safari* from Swahili).
*   **Lingua Franca:** Today, English serves as the primary global language for science, aviation, and the internet.
