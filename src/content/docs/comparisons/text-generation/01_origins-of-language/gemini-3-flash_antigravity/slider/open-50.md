The story typically begins in the 5th century with the arrival of Germanic tribes—the Angles, Saxons, and Jutes—in Roman Britain. Their dialects merged into what we now call Old English (or Anglo-Saxon). While this period established the grammatical "bones" of English, it was not a static era.
