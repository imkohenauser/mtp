English began in Britain when the **Angles, Saxons, and Jutes** migrated from areas around modern northern Germany, Denmark, and the Netherlands. Their dialects formed **Old English**. This language looked very different from today’s English: it had grammatical gender, more case endings, and freer word order. **Modern English** spread worldwide through British colonization, American influence, global trade, science, publishing, film, and the internet.
