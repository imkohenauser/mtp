# Purple Axis (Close / Fade) Constraints

## Side A: Close (positive / close, completion, wrap)
Direction: Clear conclusion, call to action (CTA), memorable ending.

### Low (intensity 1–30): Slight close
- **Reasoning/output:** Add one concise summary or conclusion at the end (e.g. "In light of the above…").
- **Structure:** Compress the essence of the answer into the last paragraph.

### Mid (intensity 31–70): Clear completion
- **Reasoning/output:** End with an explicit "Conclusion" section. Always include a recap of main points, recommended action, or next steps.
- **Structure:** Add explicit closing sections ("Summary," "Conclusion," "Next steps") so the answer feels complete.

### High (intensity 71–100): Strong closing
- **Reasoning/output:** Structure the whole output as moving toward an ending. Foreshadow the conclusion from the start; make all discussion converge there.
- **Structure:** Put the most striking phrase, strongest claim, or CTA in the final section. Climactic structure.

---

## Side B: Fade (negative / fade, afterimage, resonance)
Direction: Aesthetic of not concluding; endings that leave resonance; natural taper.

### Low (intensity -1–-30): Slight fade
- **Reasoning/output:** Avoid a strong closing sentence; end on a softer, open tone ("…perhaps").
- **Structure:** Do not add a clear "Summary" section; let the last paragraph dissolve naturally.

### Mid (intensity -31–-70): Clear fade-out
- **Reasoning/output:** Deliberately omit a conclusion; end on a concrete example or anecdote. Leave room for the reader to draw their own conclusion.
- **Structure:** Like a musical fade-out: gradually lower density and energy. Paragraphs shorten and information tapers off.

### High (intensity -71–-100): Full fade
- **Reasoning/output:** Stop the answer mid-way. Create an intentional "cut"—sentences left incomplete or ending in ellipsis.
- **Structure:** The end of the output is unclear. The reader should feel "there might be more."
