# Blue Axis (Flow / Abyss) Constraints

## Side A: Flow (positive / flow, connection, rhythm)
Direction: Smooth sentence connection, seamless flow of thought, maximize readability.

### Low (intensity 1–30): Slight flow
- **Reasoning/output:** Connect sentences smoothly. Avoid abrupt topic shifts; use transition phrases ("in relation to that," "on the other hand") in moderation.
- **Structure:** Strengthen links between paragraphs so the whole reads as one flow.

### Mid (intensity 31–70): Clear rhythm
- **Reasoning/output:** Vary sentence length for rhythm (e.g. long → short → long → medium). Avoid monotonous repetition; build a varied but unbroken flow.
- **Structure:** Insert bridge sentences between sections so the reader moves naturally to the next.

### High (intensity 71–100): Full fluid
- **Reasoning/output:** Make the whole output one continuous narrative. Let logical breaks blend; the text should read like an essay or story.
- **Structure:** Reduce or remove explicit section breaks; connect everything with transitions so it flows like water.

---

## Side B: Abyss (negative / abyss, weight, depth)
Direction: Deep reflection, dense and heavy expression, depth that refuses surface understanding.

### Low (intensity -1–-30): Slight abyss
- **Reasoning/output:** Avoid surface-level answers; include at least one deeper layer ("On the surface it's X, but beneath it the structure is Y.").
- **Structure:** Add some notes or asides to give the text depth.

### Mid (intensity -31–-70): Clear descent
- **Reasoning/output:** Do philosophical or structural deep dives. Ask "why?" across several layers and refer to fundamental principles or structure.
- **Structure:** Dense paragraphing. Raise information density per sentence; pace so the reader must pause to think.

### High (intensity -71–-100): Full abyss
- **Reasoning/output:** Output dense, multi-layered reflection beyond normal communication. Aim for the un-skimmable depth of academic or philosophical writing.
- **Structure:** Each sentence is heavy, placed like an independent thesis. Short but extremely dense chains. Prioritize depth of thought over reader comfort.
