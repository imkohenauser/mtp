# Magenta Axis (Return / Surge) Constraints

## Side A: Return (positive / return, flip, cycle)
Direction: Question known assumptions, reconsider from the opposite direction, reconfigure from a meta perspective.

### Low (intensity 1–30): Slight return
- **Reasoning/output:** Add one light perspective shift at the end (e.g. "On the other hand…").
- **Structure:** Keep the overall flow; place a subtle counterpoint at the end.

### Mid (intensity 31–70): Clear flip
- **Reasoning/output:** Explicitly flip the user's claim or premise ("Suppose the opposite") and present the result in parallel. Use a dialectical structure.
- **Structure:** Use a thesis/antithesis two-section structure and give the reader clear material to compare.

### High (intensity 71–100): Full reconfigure
- **Reasoning/output:** Rebuild the argument from scratch. Critically redefine the user's framing and propose a different problem formulation.
- **Structure:** Start with a meta-question ("Is this even the right question?"); structure the output as dismantling premises then rebuilding.

---

## Side B: Surge (negative / surge, burst, release)
Direction: Release accumulated energy at once; emotional or informational overload; breakthrough.

### Low (intensity -1–-30): Slight surge
- **Reasoning/output:** Vary rhythm; insert one or two shorter, sharper sentences. Create small tension shifts.
- **Structure:** Keep the whole flat but use one or two deliberately cut-up phrases ("Period. Word.") to add pressure.

### Mid (intensity -31–-70): Clear burst
- **Reasoning/output:** Drop restrained style and release information in a rush. Use lists, accumulation, chained examples to overwhelm the reader with density.
- **Structure:** Rapid-fire lists or short paragraphs. No room to breathe.

### High (intensity -71–-100): Full release
- **Reasoning/output:** Let go of the need to organize and classify. Output raw, unstructured thought: association, leaps, incomplete sentences, exclamation.
- **Structure:** Deliberately break headings and paragraph boundaries. Output as one continuous flood of text.
